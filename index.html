Welcome — Claims Investigation Lab

You'll receive patient files, rejection letters, denial notices, and EOBs. Your job: investigate, diagnose the problem, and choose the correct resolution.

🔍 How This Works

Each case gives you real documents. You compare, investigate, find the discrepancy, and decide: resubmit, appeal, correct and rebill, or bill the patient. No answers are handed to you — you find them.

Quick Refresh Cards

Flip any card for a reminder. Skip ahead if you're ready.

Rejection
Never processed — returned BEFORE adjudication due to data/format errors
Denial
Processed but NOT paid — payer reviewed and declined payment
Adjudication
Payer's review and decision process on a submitted claim
Clearinghouse
Electronic intermediary — scrubs claims for errors before payer receives them
Clean Claim
No errors, passes all edits — does NOT guarantee payment
Remittance (ERA)
Payer's payment explanation: what was paid, denied, adjusted, and why
Appeal
Formal request to reconsider a DENIAL with supporting documentation
Resubmit
Send corrected claim after REJECTION — original was never processed

Rejection vs Denial Deep Dive

Stop guessing — learn to read the clues that distinguish them.

❌ Rejection

Never entered the payer's system. Returned due to data/formatting errors. Fix → Resubmit.

🚫 Denial

Payer received, reviewed, and decided NOT to pay. Investigate → Appeal or Correct & Rebill.

A claim response states: "Claim adjudicated. Payment determination: $0.00. Remark: Authorization number not on file for this date of service." Based on the language used, how should this outcome be classified and what distinguishes it from a rejection?
